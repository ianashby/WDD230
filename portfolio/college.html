Photography 1 at BYU - Idaho

2020

I started photography in High School and became very familiar with the concepts behind it, but I wanted to learn more about it once I got to college. In Winter 2020, I enrolled in the Photography 1 course at BYU - Idaho. I learned a lot about the art of photography and how to use the camera to capture the best images; however, I felt that it was mostly a repeat of what I had learned in High School. I had wanted to continue on as a photography minor, but I didn't see much opportunity for me to learn more. I am very grateful for the opportunity I had to take the class as I was able to make many new friends and have my work reviewed by new people.

You may notice that a lot of these photos are in black and white. That is because we are instructed to shoot our photos in black and white to learn about exposure and lightning. I like this technique and felt that I was able to learn a lot about what it means to understand exposure. I also gained a deep appreciation for black and white photography.

Please enjoy my photos taken during Photography 1 at BYU - Idaho.
